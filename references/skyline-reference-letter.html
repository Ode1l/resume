To whom it may concern,

I am pleased to provide this reference for Jiaheng Li, who worked with Skyline Consulting Engineers Limited as a part-time Full-stack Software Developer from October 2024 to December 2025.

Skyline is a small early-stage engineering company, and we have not had a fixed in-house software or IT specialist. During Jiaheng's time with us, he was the only person responsible for software development and many practical IT matters. Although his formal role was part-time Full-stack Software Developer, in practice he took on many responsibilities similar to an IT manager.

The main project he worked on was the software development of a beam calculation book system. This was a difficult task because the calculation book came from structural engineering practice, while the software requirements had to be created almost from scratch. I was involved in defining the requirements, but because our team came from a civil and structural engineering background and had limited software knowledge, it was often difficult for us to explain what we needed in a form that could become a software product.

Jiaheng handled this communication gap very well. He spent significant time understanding how structural engineers worked, learned a considerable amount of civil engineering knowledge on his own, and translated our engineering workflow into software requirements. He then implemented the software development work independently. He also introduced us to useful engineering software tools and helped us understand basic computing concepts that were relevant to the project. His work helped us move from manual and semi-manual calculation book processes toward a more structured and usable software system.

What impressed me most was not only his technical ability, but also his communication and ownership. The project was difficult to move forward at times because we did not fully understand software development and he was the only software developer on the team. Jiaheng remained patient, asked practical questions, clarified uncertain requirements, and kept the work moving forward. He was able to work independently while still communicating clearly with people who did not have a technical background.

Beyond his technical contribution, Jiaheng was also pleasant to work with. His curiosity outside software, including history and food, made everyday collaboration with him enjoyable.

From my experience, Jiaheng is a capable and responsible developer with strong problem-solving ability, good communication skills, and the ability to learn unfamiliar domain knowledge quickly. I would confidently recommend him for software engineering or full-stack development roles, especially where the work requires independent ownership and communication between technical and non-technical people.
